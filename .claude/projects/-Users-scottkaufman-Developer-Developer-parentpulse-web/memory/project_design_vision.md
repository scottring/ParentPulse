Relish design direction decided Apr 8, 2026:

**Book metaphor replaces weather metaphor.** Weather was an experiment, didn't hold things together. The manual/book is warm and familial, not analytical.

**Bookshelf as home screen.** Family Manual (leather-bound), Growth Workbook (softer cover), Reports Binder. One main book is cleaner than many.

**Open book = landscape two-page spread.**
- Left page: Family (constellation map, insight cards) — stable/collective
- Right page: Your View (The One Thing CTA, growth progress, what's new) — dynamic/personal
- Bottom: Horizontal timeline as the spine/living TOC

**The One Thing Principle:** Every screen has one clear purpose. Action screens get one CTA. Reading screens get one focus point. When nothing is needed, say so calmly. No manufactured urgency.

**Priority hierarchy:** Unfinished business > Time-sensitive > New info from others > Synthesis ready > Growth next step > Gaps/invitations > Nothing (calm state)

**Dual lens:** Family (collective) + You (signed-in individual) layered on every screen. Same data, different reading experience per user. Always use names, never role labels.

**Pictorial over wordy:** Visual scales, icon clusters, relationship maps, color-coded cards, quote bubbles. "If you can show it as a shape, color, position, or icon — don't write a sentence."

**Why:** The app felt like a SaaS dashboard. These decisions make it feel like a warm, crafted family artifact.

**How to apply:** Every UI decision should be checked against these principles. Full details in `docs/DESIGN-VISION.md`.
