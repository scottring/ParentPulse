What it is.

Three questions, three people, ten minutes. Started because Sundays were dissolving into laundry and nobody remembered anything on Mondays.

i.

What was good this week?

Each person answers in turn. Kaleb goes first because he will not wait his turn.

ii.

What was hard?

The honest one. Nobody has to share. A pass counts as an answer.

iii.

What wants keeping into next week?

One thing — a meal, a plan, a feeling — that the family wants to carry forward. This is the line that goes into the book.

When

Sunday, 7:30pm.

After dinner, before bath. Ten minutes is enough; fifteen is better.
